Biography

A Life in Italian Opera

Donato Di Stefano studied at the Conservatorio L. Refice in Frosinone, Italy. He won numerous national and international competitions including the G. Verdi in Parma, the As.Li.Co. in Milan, the Battistini in Rieti, the Mario del Monaco in Castelfranco Veneto, and the Mario del Monaco in Marsala. He made his professional debut in 1989 in Nabucco in Rome, singing the role of Zaccaria.

A leading interpreter of buffo and bel canto repertoire, he has performed at the world's most prestigious opera houses and festivals, working with conductors including Seiji Ozawa, Riccardo Muti, Giannandrea Gavazzeni, James Levine, Nikolaus Harnoncourt, Kirill Petrenko, Zubin Mehta, Riccardo Chailly, Marc Minkowski, Bruno Campanella, Stefano Ranzani, Alberto Zedda, Rinaldo Alessandrini, and Sigiswald Kuijken, among others.

He has collaborated with directors including Dario Fo, Luc Bondy, Robert Wilson, Ferruccio Soleri, Toni Servillo, Liliana Cavani, Jack O'Brien, Jérôme Savary, Jonathan Miller, Giancarlo Del Monaco, Omri Nitzan, Grischa Asagaroff, Pier Luigi Pizzi, and Marco Arturo Marelli.
